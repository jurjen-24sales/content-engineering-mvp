# Content Scaling Strategy

Two opposing approaches to scale your warm audience across platforms. Both are valid. Choose based on resources and risk tolerance.

## Strategy 1: Depth Then Width

Maximize one platform before moving to the next.

**Steps:**
1. Post content on a relevant platform
2. Post content regularly on that platform
3. Maximize quality and quantity (short form: up to 10x/day; long form: up to 5 days/week)
4. Add another platform while maintaining the first
5. Repeat until all relevant platforms are maximized

**Advantages:**
- Audiences compound faster the more you do on one platform
- Fewer resources required
- Maximize return on effort before diversifying

**Disadvantages:**
- Less low-hanging fruit of new audiences
- No "omnipresence" feeling early on
- Single-channel risk: platforms change and can ban you for no reason

## Strategy 2: Width Then Depth

Get on every platform early, then maximize them together.

**Steps:**
1. Post content on a relevant platform
2. Post content regularly on that platform
3. Move to next relevant platform while maintaining the previous (instead of maximizing first)
4. Continue until on all relevant platforms
5. Now maximize content creation across all platforms at once

**Advantages:**
- Broader audience faster
- "Repurpose" content across platforms with minimal extra effort
- Reduced single-channel risk

**Disadvantages:**
- More labor, attention, and time required
- Risk of lots of bad content everywhere (sucky fluff)

## Benchmarks: How Well Am I Doing?

Measure monthly:

### 1. Total Followers and Reach (How Big)

| Metric | Calculation | Example |
|--------|------------|---------|
| Follower growth | End - Start | 1000 → 1500 = +500 |
| Reach growth | End - Start | 10,000 → 15,000 = +5,000 |

### 2. Rate of Growth (How Fast)

| Metric | Calculation | Example |
|--------|------------|---------|
| Growth rate | New / Started | 500 / 1000 = 50% monthly |
| Reach rate | New / Started | 5,000 / 10,000 = 50% monthly |

> "The more ways you measure, the more ways you can win." - Dr. Kashey

**Important:** Outputs only matter when inputs are consistent. Pick a posting cadence and stick with it. Hormozi posted a new podcast twice a week for four years before making the Top 100 list. It took five years to reach Top 10.

## Seven Lessons From Making Content

1. **"How I" > "How to"**: Talk about what you've done, not what others should do. "How I built my 7-figure agency" vs "How to build a 7-figure agency." When you share experience, no one can question you.

2. **We need reminding more than teaching**: 1 in 5 of Hormozi's audience didn't know he had a book despite daily posts about it. Keep repeating yourself.

3. **Puddles, Ponds, Lakes, Oceans**: Start narrow (local plumbing content), become king of the puddle, then expand to the pond (local business), lake (chains), ocean (general business).

4. **Content creates tools for salespeople**: Label "greatest hits" by problem solved and benefit provided. Sales team sends relevant content before/after calls to help prospects decide.

5. **Free content retains paying customers**: Customers include your free content when calculating ROI from your paid product. If free content is good, they stay loyal longer.

6. **Higher standards, not shorter attention spans**: People will binge 8 hours of streaming. Biology hasn't changed - they have more rewarding options. Make stuff worth binging.

7. **Avoid pre-scheduling posts**: Manual posting creates tighter feedback loops and pressure to make it better. The "submit button pressure" leads to higher quality.
